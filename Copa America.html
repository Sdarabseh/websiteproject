Copa America

"The Copa América (English: America Cup) or CONMEBOL Copa América, previously known as the South American Football Championship (Campeonato Sudamericano de Ftbol in Spanish and Copa Sul-Americana de Futebol in Portuguese), is the premier men's football event in South America. It is the world's oldest and longest-running continental football competition, as well as the third most popular. The competition decides the South American champions. Teams from North America and Asia have been invited to compete since the 1990s."Wikepdia

"Since 1993, the event has featured a total of 12 teams, including all ten CONMEBOL nations as well as two teams from other confederations. Except for 1999, when AFC participant Japan completed up the 12-team roster, and 2019, which included Japan and Qatar, Mexico competed in every tournament from 1993 through 2016, with one additional team picked from CONCACAF. The Copa América Centenario, which took place in 2016, featured 16 teams, including six from CONCACAF and ten from CONMEBOL. Mexico's two second-place finishes are the most by a non-CONMEBOL team."Wikepdia

"Since the tournament's inception in 1916, eight of the 10 CONMEBOL national sides have won at least once, with just Ecuador and Venezuela remaining undefeated. With 15 cups apiece, Argentina and Uruguay hold the record for most championships in the tournament's history. Argentina has hosted the event the most times, having hosted the first edition in 1916.
